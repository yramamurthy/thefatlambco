How to select Mecheri lambs for fattening at sandhai

Mecheri fattening6 min read

Profit in Mecheri fattening starts at purchase. A cheap lamb with poor frame, disease risk, or wrong age can become expensive after 30 days of feed. The goal at sandhai is not to buy the cheapest animal; it is to buy the animal that can convert feed into saleable weight with the least risk.

What to look for first

FrameLong body, good chest width, straight back, strong legs. Avoid narrow, stunted lambs.
AgePrefer young growing lambs, not old animals disguised as feeders. Check teeth and body frame together.
HealthBright eyes, alert, clean nose, normal breathing, firm dung, no lameness.
Breed typeFor Mecheri, look for clean body type, hardy frame, and animals that suit your local buyer preference.

Red flags

  • Heavy coughing, nasal discharge, dullness, swollen jaw, loose dung, limping, bottle jaw, wounds, or bloated belly.
  • Animals that look full only because they drank water or were fed just before sale.
  • Mixed unknown animals from many sources without any health history.
  • Very thin lambs that need rescue feeding; they may not respond fast enough for a fattening cycle.

Price discipline

Before entering sandhai, fix your maximum purchase price based on expected exit weight, expected sale price, mortality allowance, and feed cost. If the price crosses your calculation, walk away. Fattening profit is made by buying right, not by hoping the market will save a bad purchase.

After purchase

Do not send new Mecheri lambs directly into the main batch. Quarantine, observe appetite, shift feed slowly, and identify weak animals early. A good buying day can be spoiled by poor arrival management.

Recommendation: choose medium-framed, healthy, growing Mecheri lambs with clean breathing, strong legs, and enough frame to add weight. Avoid bargain animals with health doubts. The right lamb is the one that reaches target sale weight with fewer treatment days.
